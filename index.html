Frequently Asked Questions

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) pipes are thermoplastic pipes known for their high strength-to-density ratio. They are manufactured from petroleum-based polyethylene material and are widely used for water supply, gas distribution, sewage systems, and industrial applications. Their flexibility, corrosion resistance, and long service life make them superior to traditional piping materials.

HDPE pipes offer numerous advantages: superior chemical resistance, leak-proof fusion joints, exceptional flexibility, 50+ year service life, lightweight for easy handling, corrosion-free operation, and environmental sustainability through 100% recyclability.

HDPE pipes are manufactured through an extrusion process. Raw polyethylene pellets are melted and pushed through a die to form the pipe shape. The extruded pipe then passes through vacuum sizing, water cooling, and haul-off units before being cut to specified lengths or wound into coils.

HDPE pipes are used in potable water supply, sewage and drainage systems, gas distribution, industrial process piping, mining slurry transport, irrigation systems, telecom ducting, marine outfall systems, and landfill leachate collection.

Yes, HDPE pipes can be extensively customized in terms of diameter (20mm to 1600mm), pressure ratings, wall thickness, coil lengths, and color coding. We also offer custom fittings and accessories to meet specific project requirements.
